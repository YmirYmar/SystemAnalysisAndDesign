# SystemAnalysisAndDesign
System Analysis And Design Portfolio Prep for SNHU CS-255

# Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
The DriverPass project's purpose was to gain requirements from a client and use those requirements to build a system that provides students with the ability to access online classes, practice tests, and schedule appointments. The client in the project was the Owner of DriverPass, Liam. His main goal for the system was to create this online environment for DriverPass to fill the void in the market when it comes to training students for the driving test at their local DMV. Some of his requirements includes that the system is web-based/cloud-based and works functionally with the internet, recieves automatic updates from the local DMV, and there is course materials and assessments as well as the ability to create accounts for the students and track all appointments and have a changelog. The Owner also wanted full access over these accounts, had there been any need to remove or change passwords for them.

# What did you do particularly well?
I believe that I followed through and met with all my business requirements, listed on the "CS 255 Business Requiremtns Document.docx" for DriverPass. By building mulitple UML diagrams, I was able to implement the requirements listed by my client while showing all the necessary information to create the system. 

# If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
I think that I could have created a better UML Sequence Diagram. I think that the diagram follows more of an activty diagram rather than a true sequence diagram. I could focus more on object interactions over time and include lifelines and messages to follow proper UML standards. 

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
Throughout this project, I interpreted the user's needs through an interview with the Owner and documentation of their requirements, and implemented them into my design by meeting those requirements and building a system around them. It's incredibly important to listen to the user's needs when designing their product because theyr satisfaction is key and success of the product depends on that satisfaction.

By having an in-person interview with the client, similar to the DriverPass interview, I will be able to map out the product desired by my client and work in tandem with what they need and want in the product. I will use these skills developed in this course to map out and create a clear path for my designs and software.
